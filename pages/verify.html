Paste the credential JSON

Drop the .json file from the licence email below, or paste it directly. The verifier recomputes the SHA-256 proof in your browser — nothing is uploaded anywhere.

How verification works

Every licence is a JSON-LD document with a proof field containing a SHA-256 hash. The hash is computed across the canonicalised credential body (every field, every nested value, sorted by key).

To tamper is to break

Change any value in the credential — one digit in the licence ID, one letter in the school name, one day on the validity window — and the recomputed hash will no longer match the stored proofValue. Verification fails. The fraud is visible.

What the verifier checks

  1. The credential is well-formed JSON-LD with a proof object
  2. The proof.type is Sha256Stamp2026
  3. Recomputing the SHA-256 of the canonicalised body yields the same proofValue
  4. Reports issuer entity, validity dates, and the holder

Production roadmap

For the founding cohort (2026), Sha256Stamp2026 is a content hash — tamper-evident but not unforgeable. The 2027 issuance schedule moves to Ed25519Signature2020 signed by the issuing entity’s registered DID, fully unforgeable. The verifier here will keep working for both.
