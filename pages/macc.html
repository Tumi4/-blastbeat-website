The Events

Five Types of Show. One Goal.

Students choose their event format — and run the entire business around it. Every event is student-produced, student-marketed, and student-managed.

Headline Event
Live Concert
Headline acts, supporting slots, full production. Students book talent, negotiate contracts, and manage the night.
Competition
Talent Show
School-wide auditions, judging panels, prize money. Students run the entire casting and show process.
Weekly Income
Open Mic Night
Regular revenue stream. Students manage the sign-up, sound, promotion, and door takings week after week.
Arts & Culture
Creative Showcase
Visual art, dance, spoken word, film. Students curate and produce a ticketed arts evening.
Fan Favourite
Battle of the Bands
The classic Blastbeat format. Live bands, audience vote, prize pot. The event that started it all in 2003.
Content Creation
Blastbeat TV
10-minute episode filmed and submitted to the national competition at the end of the programme.

The Money

What Happens to the Revenue?

Every rand, pound, or euro your team earns is 100% real. After costs, the net profit is split two ways — your team keeps the majority, and the rest goes to a student-designed climate action project.
